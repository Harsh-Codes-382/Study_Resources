# 02 — Types of Networks by Area

Networks can be divided based on their **area of distribution**. From smallest to largest:

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    PAN[PAN - ~10m] --> HAN[HAN - one house]
    HAN --> LAN[LAN - office/school]
    LAN --> CAN[CAN - campus]
    CAN --> MAN[MAN - city]
    MAN --> WAN[WAN - cities/countries]
    WAN --> GAN[GAN - global via satellite]
```

## The families

| Type | Full Form | Range / Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **PAN** | Personal Area Network | Up to **~10 meters**. Personal devices — computers, phones, fax, printers. |
| **LAN** | Local Area Network | Small geographical location — office, hospital, school. |
| **HAN** | House Area Network | A LAN used *within a house* — connects home devices like PCs, phones, printers. |
| **CAN** | Campus Area Network | Connection of devices within a campus, linking departments of the same organization. |
| **MAN** | Metropolitan Area Network | Spans large cities — devices across a wide geographical area within a metro. |
| **WAN** | Wide Area Network | Spans wide geographical locations — can connect cities and countries. |
| **GAN** | Global Area Network | Uses **satellites** to connect devices across the globe. |

## How to think about it

- **PAN** is you and your Bluetooth earbuds.
- **HAN** is your Wi-Fi router at home.
- **LAN** is your office building's wired/Wi-Fi network.
- **CAN** is a college campus tying multiple LANs together.
- **MAN** is a city-scale network — a whole metro area under one operator.
- **WAN** is the internet backbone — anything spanning cities/countries.
- **GAN** is satellite-linked, planet-scale reach.
